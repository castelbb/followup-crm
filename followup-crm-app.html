🌻 Granola note guide Show ▼

Use these headings in Granola for the best email results. The more detail you add under each, the more personalised your emails will be.

## Who they are
Role, how long they've been there, personality read from the call — analytical, decisive, cautious, enthusiastic?
"Susan is a new hire, 3 weeks in, brought in specifically to fix the expense process. Highly motivated. Tyler defers to her on the day-to-day."
## Their world before us
What does their day actually look like dealing with this problem? Be specific — what breaks, who feels it, how often, what does it cost them in time or stress?
"Drivers 3 hours away forget receipts. Kem manually enters every charge into QuickBooks as a single consolidated entry. Charlotte does everything right and the Expensify connector breaks anyway."
## What lit them up
Specific moments in the demo where they leaned in, asked follow-up questions, or said something positive. Quote them if possible.
"Eyes lit up at SMS submission — 'so they don't even need to download anything?' Asked twice about the real-time notification."
## What they're worried about
Hesitations raised, risks they see. Brian addresses these personally — don't repeat in emails.
"Concerned about vendor duplication in QuickBooks. Worried the integration will break like Expensify. App Store reviews came up."
## How we solve it
Which specific features map directly to their pain. Connect each feature to a problem they named.
"Yodlee connection solves the duplicate card feed issue. Vendor drop-down restriction keeps QuickBooks clean. Custom CSV export handles Lightspeed."
## The politics
Who else is involved in the decision, who has influence, who needs to say yes, who might block it.
"Tyler owns the decision but defers to Susan. Partners need to sign off — 2 of them. CFO not on the call."
## Next steps agreed
Word for word what was agreed at the end of the call — who does what by when.
"Brian to send pricing summary. Jane to check Expensify contract terms and confirm with partners. Follow up in 1 week."
